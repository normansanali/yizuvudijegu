Early Life

Shannon was born on November 29, 1985; and his full name is Waipahu Shannon Brown. He attended the Proviso East High School in Maywood.

Basketball was his passion during school and college, and in 2003, he was named Illinois Mr. Basketball; as well as a McDonald’s All-American. In 2003 itself, he was listed as the #1 shooting guard and the #3 player in the nation; by the website rivals.com

Later, Brown played college basketball for the Michigan State University. He was a second-team All-Big Ten as a junior, and later an All-Big Ten Defensive selection.

Career

Brown got drafted in the first round of the 2006 NBA draft, with the 25th pick by the team Cleveland Cavaliers. He could play in only 23 games as his progress was adversely affected by a shin injury. However, he displayed promise and continued to be retained by the Cleveland Cavaliers till 2008.

In February 2008, Brown was traded to the Chicago Bulls team, and he stayed with them till August when he signed for the Charlotte Bobcats for $0.8 million minimum NBA salary for one year. During February 2009, he was traded to the Los Angeles Lakers, with whom he performed well.

In July 2009, Brown had a $4.2 million contract for his return to the Los Angeles Lakers for two years.

In August 2010, Brown signed a $4.6 million contract with the Los Angeles Lakers for two-year. In the season he did quite well by scoring a season-high of 21 points against top teams.

In July 2012, Brown signed $7 million contracts of two years with Phoenix Suns. Brown kept up his good performance with a career-high 32 points. He also scored well against his former teams.

In October 2013, Brown was traded to the Washington Wizards. In February 2014, Brown signed a 10-day contract with the San Antonio Spurs, and a few days later signed another 10-day contract.

In February and March 2014, he signed a couple of 10-day contract with the New York Knicks. Later in March; he was signed for the rest of the season. In August 2014, the Miami Heat signed him for the rest of the season.

In 2017, Brown was added to the Milwaukee Bucks G-League affiliate roster of the Wisconsin Herd; and in January 2018, he was traded to the Delaware Blue Coats, it is a team of the NBA G League.
