How did Bonnie Bernstein start her Professional Career?

Bonnie Bernstein’s early career highlights include serving as the news and sports director for WXJN-FM radio in Delaware. She stayed there for three productive years before leaving for her current position as weekend news anchor at Bonnie Bernstein station WMDT-TV.

Formerly known for her work on multiple networks, she is now best known for her role as Campus Insiders’ vice president of content and brand development and the network’s face.

In 1995, Bernstein made a pivotal career move by accepting a position with ESPN as the Chicago Bureau Chief. She was tasked with covering the historic championship run of Michael Jordan and the Chicago Bulls.

She then joined CBS Sports, where she covered the NCAA Men’s Basketball Championship as a lead sideline reporter before moving on to the NFL. In January 2006, after a positive experience, Bonnie came back to work at ESPN.

Before gaining the lucrative position of vice president at Campus Insiders, which she maintained for over a decade, Bonnie didn’t limit herself to working only on television; she also appeared on radio shows on 1050 ESPN Radio.
