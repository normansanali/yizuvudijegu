Sandlot Where Are They Now?

August 2024 · 5 minute read

The 1993 film “The Sandlot” is a beloved classic that has captured the hearts of audiences for decades. It’s a nostalgic look back at the summer of 1962 through the eyes of a group of young baseball players. As the years have passed, fans of the film have often wondered what happened to the cast of characters who brought the story to life. In this article, we’ll explore where the actors from “The Sandlot” are now, delving into their lives and careers since the film’s release.

Tom Guiry as Scotty Smalls

Tom Guiry, who played the new kid in town, Scotty Smalls, has continued acting since his memorable role in “The Sandlot.” His post-Sandlot career includes appearances in films like “Black Hawk Down” and “Mystic River.” Despite some legal troubles and a quieter career in recent years, Guiry has maintained a presence in the acting world with roles in various TV shows and movies.

Mike Vitar as Benjamin Franklin Rodriguez

Mike Vitar, who portrayed the baseball prodigy Benny “The Jet” Rodriguez, retired from acting in 1997. After leaving Hollywood, Vitar became a firefighter in Los Angeles. His life took a challenging turn when he faced legal issues in 2015, but he continues to serve as a firefighter.

Patrick Renna as Hamilton “Ham” Porter

Patrick Renna’s portrayal of the wisecracking Hamilton “Ham” Porter remains one of his most iconic roles. Renna has remained active in the entertainment industry, with roles in various TV shows and independent films. He’s also active on social media, often engaging with fans and sharing memories from his “Sandlot” days.

Chauncey Leopardi as Michael “Squints” Palledorous

Chauncey Leopardi, known for his role as the lovable Squints, has continued to act sporadically. He’s appeared in TV shows like “Gilmore Girls” and movies such as “The Opposite of Sex.” Outside of acting, Leopardi has embraced a quiet life and occasionally attends “Sandlot” reunions.

Marty York as Alan “Yeah-Yeah” McClennan

Marty York, who played the enthusiastic Yeah-Yeah, has had a few acting roles since “The Sandlot.” He’s also become a personal trainer and has worked to maintain a fit lifestyle, which he showcases on his social media platforms.

Brandon Adams as Kenny DeNunez

Brandon Adams, who brought Kenny DeNunez to life, continued acting into the 2000s with roles in both film and television. He’s also pursued a career in music and remains connected to his “Sandlot” roots by attending fan events.

Grant Gelt as Bertram Grover Weeks

Grant Gelt, who played the bespectacled Bertram, stepped away from acting in the early 2000s. He has since moved into the music industry, working behind the scenes in artist management and music production.

Shane Obedzinski as Tommy “Repeat” Timmons

Shane Obedzinski, known for his role as the younger Timmons brother, has largely left acting behind. He now owns a pizza restaurant in Florida and occasionally makes appearances at “Sandlot” related events.

Victor DiMattia as Timmy Timmons

Victor DiMattia, who played Tommy’s older brother Timmy, has taken on a few acting roles since “The Sandlot.” He’s also explored directing and podcasting, showcasing his love for the entertainment industry in various forms.

Marley Shelton as Wendy Peffercorn

Marley Shelton, who captured hearts as the lifeguard Wendy Peffercorn, has enjoyed a successful acting career. She’s appeared in films like “Sin City” and “Planet Terror,” and continues to work in both film and television.

James Earl Jones as Mr. Mertle

The legendary James Earl Jones, who played the blind former baseball player Mr. Mertle, has never left the spotlight. With a career spanning over seven decades, Jones has continued to act in film, television, and theater, and remains one of the most respected actors in the industry.

Denis Leary as Bill

Denis Leary, who portrayed Scotty’s stepfather Bill, has had a prolific career in comedy, film, and television. He’s best known for his lead role in the TV series “Rescue Me” and continues to be an influential figure in the entertainment world.

Karen Allen as Mom

Karen Allen, who played Scotty’s understanding mother, has continued her acting career with roles in film and theater. She reprised her famous role as Marion Ravenwood in “Indiana Jones and the Kingdom of the Crystal Skull” and remains active in the acting community.

Art LaFleur as The Babe

Art LaFleur, who made a memorable appearance as The Babe, had a successful career as a character actor. He passed away in 2021, leaving behind a legacy of diverse roles in film and television.
