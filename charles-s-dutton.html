Charles Stanley Dutton (born January 30 1951) is an American actor as well as director , and producer. He is most well-known for his work on the TV series Roc (1991-1994) as well as the film for television The Piano Lesson (1995) The latter was a winner of an Golden Globe Award nomination. His other awards are the award of three Primetime Emmy Awards and three NAACP Image Awards.

After the knife fight Dutton was convicted in 1967 to manslaughter. He was sentenced to five-year prison. He began working at the Maryland House of Correction in Jessup, Maryland. After about 18 or twenty months of imprisonment, Dutton was questioned on charges of robbery and assault with a handgun. He was convicted of the gun violation and taken into the Maryland Penitentiary, near his childhood home, for three years. A dispute with a guard snared an additional eight years. In the context of the incident, Dutton later said, “I got three years for killing a black man and eight for punching a white man.”

Several months into his second prison term, Dutton was sentenced to six days of solitary confinement for refusing to clean toilets. Prisoners were allowed to take one book and, by accident, he grabbed an anthology of black playwrights. He enjoyed the book so much that upon release from solitary he petitioned the warden to start a drama group for the winter talent show. The warden agreed on the condition that Dutton go back to school and get his GED. Dutton accomplished that and eventually completed a two-year college program at Hagerstown Junior College (now Hagerstown Community College) in Hagerstown, Maryland, graduating with an Associate of Arts degree in 1976.
